Little known facts about cats.

Cats are one of the human-friendly pets, but there's a lot about this pet that you don't know.

  • Cats don't like sweetness.
  • Sleep accounts for 70% of a cat's life.
  • The longest cat in the world ever measured was 48.5 inches (1.23 m).

According to a study, cats recognize their owners' voices, but they appear to be quite quiet.

The ancestral wildcat genome was significantly altered during domestication.

During domestication, cats do not undergo many changes, so they are still able to survive when they return to the wild. Some of the wildcats' natural behaviors and characteristics may have been pre-adapted to them for domestication.

Outstanding biological characteristics.

Cats have excellent night vision, domestic cats' hearing is most acute in the frequency range of 500 Hz to 32 kHz, and cats have a keen sense of smell, partly due to their well-developed, surface organs. mucosa is large (about 5.8 cm2 wide, twice that of a human).

The special relationship between cats and humans.

Cats can distinguish when their owners are talking in a voice directed toward them from someone else's, but do not react differently when a stranger changes their tone. One-on-one relationships are important for cats and humans to form strong relationships.

Cat's way of life.

Cats like high temperatures and sleep a lot (average 13-14 hours per day). Except for the breeding season, cats live alone. The male feline produces an attractive odor and meows earnestly to seduce the male cat, but after mating, she repels the male cat.
